<Section icon={Shield} title="The Legacy Piece Haki System Overview">

In the world of **Legacy Piece**, Haki serves as the ultimate equalizer. While Devil Fruits provide flashy abilities and elemental transformations, Haki is the fundamental force that allows a player to transcend their natural limits. Whether you are a swordsman or a fruit user, mastering **legacy piece haki** is not optional—it is a requirement for anyone looking to survive the late-game islands and challenging boss encounters.

The system is currently divided into two primary disciplines: Armament (Busoshoku) and Observation (Kenbunshoku). Each serves a distinct purpose in your build. Armament Haki is your primary tool for dealing damage to "Elemental" or "Logia" users who would otherwise be invincible to physical attacks. Observation Haki, on the other hand, is your primary defensive layer, granting you the "Mantra" to see through enemy movements.

<Section icon={Target} title="Unlocking Busoshoku Haki: Armament Mastery">

Busoshoku Haki, often referred to as Armament Haki, is the most common form used by players in **Legacy Piece**. When activated, it typically coats your limbs or weapons in a dark, metallic sheen. This coating significantly increases your physical damage and allows your swords or fists to interact with the "true body" of elemental fruit users.

To unlock this ability, you must embark on a specific progression path. Unlike basic combat skills, Haki requires a combination of Beli (in-game currency) and a successful completion of a trainer's challenge. This often involves defeating a series of enemies or surviving a timed trial to prove your worthiness.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Mastering Kenbunshoku Haki: Observation Skills">

Kenbunshoku Haki, or Observation Haki, is the pinnacle of defensive utility in **Legacy Piece**. Instead of hardening your body, this Haki sharpens your senses to a supernatural degree. For many players, this is the "dodge mechanic" that allows them to survive high-damage combos from bosses.

When active, Kenbunshoku Haki allows your character to automatically evade a set number of attacks. These "dodges" are represented by a meter or a set of charges on your UI. As you level up this form of **legacy piece haki**, the number of charges increases, and the cooldown between dodges decreases.

| Mastery Tier | Dodge Charges | Additional Effects |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic** | 2-3 Dodges | Detects nearby player names through walls |
| **Intermediate** | 5-6 Dodges | Shows enemy health bars and current stamina |
| **Advanced** | 8+ Dodges | Increased dodge window and reduced spirit drain |

<Callout type="tip" title="Mastery Strategy">
To train Observation Haki efficiently, let low-level NPCs attack you while Haki is active. Every successful auto-dodge grants a small amount of Haki Experience.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="Spirit Drain">
Kenbunshoku Haki consumes "Spirit" or "Stamina" rapidly. If your meter hits zero, your Haki will break, leaving you vulnerable and stunned for a short duration.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Haki Training Routes & Leveling Guide">

Unlocking Haki is only the beginning. To truly become a powerhouse in **Legacy Piece**, you must grind your Mastery levels. Efficient training routes involve finding clusters of NPCs that attack quickly, allowing you to maximize the number of Haki interactions per minute.

The best locations for training depend on your current level and the type of Haki you are focusing on. For Armament, you want enemies with high health so you can hit them repeatedly. For Observation, you want enemies with fast attack speeds to trigger dodges.

| Island | Recommended Level | Best Haki Focus |
