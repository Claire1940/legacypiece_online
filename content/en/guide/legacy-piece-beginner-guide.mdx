<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Starter Progression Route">

To level up efficiently, you must follow a structured quest route starting on Fusha Island. Quests are marked by colored exclamation points above NPC heads, indicating different mission types. 

| Quest Type | Marker Color | Description & Priority | EXP Yield |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Story Quests** | Blue | Main storyline progression; should always be completed first | High |
| **Side Quests** | Golden / Yellow | Repeatable missions used to bridge level gaps before bosses | Medium |
| **Boss Quests** | Red | Challenging fights that unlock travel to subsequent islands | Very High |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Redeem Codes and Claim Rewards">
    As soon as you spawn, open the menu and redeem all active codes to secure starting Beli and free spins. Claim any available group rewards to boost your starting wallet.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Activate the Fusha Spawn Crystal">
    Walk up to the green Spawn Crystal outside near the Party's Bar and hold E to activate it. This ensures you spawn nearby if you are defeated by early-game bandits.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Complete Woop Slap's Quest">
    Locate Woop Slap standing near the windmill. Accept his introductory quest and follow the brown quest marker on your screen to learn the layout of the island.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Grind at the Party's Bar">
    Head inside the Party's Bar and speak to Makino. Accept her story quests to defeat local bandits, repeating them until you reach Level 15.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Purchase the Katana">
    Once you have accumulated enough Beli from quests, visit the local weapon shop and purchase the Katana to replace your default Fist Fighting Style.
  </Step>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Unlocking Fighting Styles and Devil Fruits">

While the default Fist Fighting Style works for early-game bandits, you will need to acquire advanced weapons, specialized fighting styles, and Devil Fruits to progress through mid-game content.

### Early Weapon and Style Trainers
You can purchase basic weapons from shops or farm bosses for rare drops. Specialized fighting styles require finding trainers hidden across different islands.

- **One Sword Style (1SS)**: Located on Mystery Island. You must defeat three students and complete weight-training tasks to unlock it.
- **Blackleg Style**: Located on Shell's Town. This style focuses on powerful kick combos and high mobility.
- **Electro Style**: Exclusive to the Mink race, offering lightning-infused melee attacks.

### Devil Fruit Farming
Devil Fruits grant powerful elemental or physical abilities. In Legacy Piece, Devil Fruits spawn naturally under trees across all islands every 40 minutes. Fusha Island's forest is highly recommended for fruit hunting due to its dense tree population.

| Fruit | Rarity | Primary Use Case | Grinding Efficiency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light** | Rare | Ranged attacks, extremely high mobility, fast travel | ★★★★★ |
| **Magma** | Rare | Massive area-of-effect (AoE) damage, high burn damage | ★★★★★ |
| **Ice** | Uncommon | Crowd control, freezes water to walk on seas | ★★★★☆ |
| **Fire** | Uncommon | Balanced early-game damage, solid burn DOT | ★★★☆☆ |

<Callout type="success" title="Ideal Grinding Setup">
Aiming for either **Magma** or **Light** is the best long-term strategy for beginners. Both fruits allow you to clear large groups of quest mobs simultaneously, drastically reducing your total leveling time.
</Callout>
